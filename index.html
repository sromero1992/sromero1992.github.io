About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Single-Cell Data Core (SCDC) at Texas A&M University, where I bridge quantum computing, high-performance computing (HPC), and life sciences data analytics. My work focuses on developing scalable hybrid quantum-classical algorithms for computationally hard, data-intensive problems — with current applications in single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) for cancer research.


I hold a Ph.D. in Computational Science from The University of Texas at El Paso (UTEP), where my dissertation focused on advances in one-electron self-interaction-correction methods for density functional theory. My research integrates Quantum Annealing (QA) and gate-based quantum algorithms (Qiskit) to solve NP-hard optimization and classification problems, with expertise spanning quantum chemistry, bioinformatics, and HPC.

Research Interests

My research philosophy centers on creating scalable hybrid quantum-classical solutions for computationally hard, data-intensive problems. I leverage quantum architectures through dense data encoding to achieve breakthroughs in optimization and statistical modeling that purely classical computing cannot reach.

⚛ Quantum Annealing & Optimization

Designing QUBO models for NP-hard problems including feature selection in high-dimensional bioinformatics datasets and network analysis problems.

🔬 Gate-Based Quantum Algorithms

Developing advanced quantum circuits using Qiskit for dense data encoding, quantum kernels (QML), and variational algorithms (VQE, QAOA) for classification and optimization.

🖥 HPC & Quantum-Classical Integration

Investigating algorithmic specifications for efficiently integrating quantum processors with classical supercomputers using Fortran/MPI and C++/OpenMP frameworks.

🧬 Single-Cell Genomics

Applying quantum algorithms to scRNA-seq data for cancer cell classification, feature selection, and understanding cell differentiation and drug delivery mechanisms.

⚗ Density Functional Theory

Expert in PZSIC, LSIC, and FLOSIC methods for self-interaction-corrected DFT. Five years of Fortran90 code development for high-performance molecular simulation.

🔐 Quantum Security

Exploring foundational work in quantum secure protocols using algorithms like Shor's and Grover's to secure data channels within HPC frameworks.
